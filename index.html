What is Basal Metabolic Rate?

  • Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) calculates the energy a person uses while at rest
  • Most calories burned on a daily basis come from Basal metabolism
  • BMR is only an approximation, as it does not take into account lean body mass and body fat
  • However, a person can better plan there diet and workout routines if they have an approximate idea of their BMR
  • Several methods for estimating BMR exist, with the Harris-Benedict equation (1919) being the most famous
  • The Mifflin St Jeor Equation for predicting BMR was created in 1990, and studies have shown it to be a more accurate estimator of BMR

How is BMR calculated?

This app will be using the Mifflin St Jeor Equation for BMR:

\[\frac{10m}{1kg} + \frac{6.25h}{1cm} - \frac{5a}{1 year} + s\]

\[\text{$m$ is weight}\] \[\text{$h$ is height}\] \[\text{$a$ is age}\] \[\text{$s$ is sex}\] \[\text{if $s$ is male, $s$ is 5}\] \[\text{if $s$ is female, $s$ is -161}\]

An example:

We can use this equation to calculate the BMR for a 25 year old male, that weighs 65kg, and it 175cm tall.

\[\frac{10*65kg}{1kg} + \frac{6.25*175cm}{1cm} - \frac{5*25 years}{1 year} + 5\]
